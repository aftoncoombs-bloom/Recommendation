# Qgiv forms

1. The number of required and optional fields is inversely correlated to the conversion rate by increasing the resistance to completing the form.
2. The number of restrictions when used with the multirestriction system is inversely correlated to the conversion rate by increasing the resistance to completing the form.
3. The number of restrictions is positively correlated to transaction volume to a point by motivating greater amounts when the donor feels like they have some control over how their gift is used.
4. Giving events an early registration deadline that pre-dates the event itself motivates registrations by adding a sense of urgency.
5. Placing the payment information control at the top of the form increases conversion rates by creating a sense of commitment after the donor has already entered their credit card information.
6. Conversion rates are positively correlated with the number of available payment options by maximizing the possibility of making the donor's preferred payment option available for them to use.
7. The implementation of mobile VT's by the organization increases donation volume by giving the donors maximum opportunities to give.

# Hobnob events

1. Gamifying fundraising by way of using badges motivates participants to more aggressively pursue donations and raise more donations.
2. Teams increase fundraising volumes by increasing the participation of event registrants.
3. Custom "thank you" notifications increase participation with the event by creating a sense of true and personal appreciation in the minds of the recipients.
4. Conversion rates and volumes are positively correlated to CMS customization as it increases the familiarity and sense of legitimacy of the event pages for the participants and donors.
5. Inreased social media sharing by participants is positively correlated with fundraising volume.
6. Team and participant customization of fundraising pages is positively correlated with conversion rates for donors to teams and participants.
7. Team and registration limits are inversely correlated to funds raised by limiting the number of people who can participate in the fundraising.
